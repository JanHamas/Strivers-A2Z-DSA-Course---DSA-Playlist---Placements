
# 🚀 DSA MASTER ROADMAP (Python)

## 🧠 Phase 0: Mindset (VERY important)

Before touching DSA, lock this in:

* DSA = **thinking + patterns**, not memorization
* Struggle is **normal** (even seniors struggle)
* 1 problem deeply > 10 problems shallowly
* Write code by hand → then optimize

📌 **Rule**: If you can *explain* your solution, you’ve learned it.

## ⏱ Phase 2: Time & Space Complexity (3–4 days)

Here are **five excellent books** to master **Data Structures & Algorithms (DSA) with Python** — covering everything from beginner-friendly to deep, interview-focused texts 👇 ([LinkedIn][1])

### 📘 1. *Problem Solving with Algorithms and Data Structures Using Python*

**Authors:** Bradley N. Miller & David L. Ranum
✅ Great for beginners or anyone who wants Python-specific, hands-on learning with lots of examples.
📌 Covers arrays, stacks, queues, trees, sorting, searching, recursion, and more — all in Python. ([GeeksforGeeks][2])

---

### 📗 2. *Data Structures and Algorithms in Python*

**Authors:** Michael T. Goodrich, Roberto Tamassia & Michael H. Goldwasser
⭐ A widely recommended comprehensive textbook that explains core DSA concepts and implementations in Python.
📌 A solid next step after basics — good depth and canonical explanations. ([LinkedIn][1])

---

### 📙 3. *Grokking Algorithms: An Illustrated Guide for Programmers and Other Curious People*

**Author:** Aditya Bhargava
👍 Excellent visual and intuitive introduction if you’re new to algorithms.
📌 Uses friendly diagrams and simple Python code to explain key ideas like binary search, recursion, greedy, and dynamic programming. ([Tableau][3])

---

### 📕 4. *A Common-Sense Guide to Data Structures and Algorithms in Python*

**Author:** Jay Wengrow
✅ Practical and approachable — finds the balance between theory and coding.
📌 Great if books full of math feel intimidating; builds intuition around patterns and problem-solving. ([reddit.com][4])

---

### 📓 5. *Cracking the Coding Interview*

**Author:** Gayle Laakmann McDowell
🎯 Not Python-only, but *essential* for interviews.
📌 Combines interview strategies with DSA practice across topics and real coding questions. ([GeeksforGeeks][5])

---
